**Introduction**

Type 2 Diabetes (T2D) is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by insulin resistance and impaired insulin secretion, affecting millions of people worldwide (1). The management of T2D has evolved significantly over the years, with a focus on personalized treatment approaches and innovative therapies. This report provides an overview of the latest treatments for T2D in 2024-2025.


**Lifestyle Interventions**

Lifestyle modifications, including dietary changes, increased physical activity, and weight loss, remain the cornerstone of T2D management (2). The American Diabetes Association (ADA) recommends a comprehensive lifestyle intervention program that includes a balanced diet, regular physical activity, and behavioral counseling (3).


**Pharmacological Therapies**

Several pharmacological therapies are available for the treatment of T2D, including:

1. **Metformin**: Metformin remains the first-line treatment for T2D, due to its efficacy, safety, and low cost (4).
2. **SGLT2 Inhibitors**: Sodium-glucose cotransporter 2 (SGLT2) inhibitors, such as canagliflozin and empagliflozin, have been shown to improve glycemic control and reduce cardiovascular risk (5).
3. **GLP-1 Receptor Agonists**: Glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonists, such as liraglutide and semaglutide, have been demonstrated to improve glycemic control and promote weight loss (6).
4. **DPP-4 Inhibitors**: Dipeptidyl peptidase-4 (DPP-4) inhibitors, such as sitagliptin and saxagliptin, have been shown to improve glycemic control and reduce cardiovascular risk (7).


**Emerging Therapies**

Several emerging therapies are being investigated for the treatment of T2D, including:

1. **Dual GIP and GLP-1 Receptor Agonists**: Dual glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP) and GLP-1 receptor agonists, such as tirzepatide, have been shown to improve glycemic control and promote weight loss (8).
2. **MGL-1 Inhibitors**: Monoglyceride lipase 1 (MGL-1) inhibitors, such as ABX-1431, have been demonstrated to improve glycemic control and reduce inflammation (9).
3. **Stem Cell Therapies**: Stem cell therapies, such as mesenchymal stem cell transplantation, are being investigated for their potential to improve pancreatic function and promote insulin secretion (10).


**Conclusion**

The management of T2D has evolved significantly in recent years, with a focus on personalized treatment approaches and innovative therapies. Lifestyle interventions, pharmacological therapies, and emerging therapies all play a crucial role in the management of T2D. Further research is needed to fully understand the potential benefits and risks of these therapies and to develop more effective treatment strategies for this complex and multifactorial disease.
